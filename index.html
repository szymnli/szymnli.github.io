Who I Am

I study cybersecurity engineering at Wrocław University of Science and Technology. The program covers the usual suspects: network security, OS internals, PKI, forensics, cryptography. It's decent on theory; I try to make up the gap on the practical side.

Most of what I've learned about security came from building things and breaking them in VMs. Writing a Unix shell in C teaches you more about process management than any lecture. Same with building a NIDS — you start caring a lot more about TCP flags.

I run an Arch-based Linux daily, use QEMU/KVM for lab work, and have a Windows dual-boot for when I need it. My current focus is IAM at Schaeffler during the day and Lynx (a HIDS daemon) in the evenings.
